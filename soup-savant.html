Soup Savant
A Homebrew Class

Every soup savant is chasing the same thing: a soup so perfect that anyone who tastes it would weep — not from sadness, but from the sudden and overwhelming sense that they have finally tasted exactly what they needed. No savant has cooked it yet. Every one of them believes they will be first.

In the Field

The halfling had been cornered for ten minutes before the wolf pack finally broke ranks. It was not a sword that drove them off. It was the smell — a slow-simmered fish stew wafting from the thermos at his hip, so deeply and impossibly good that the wolves sat down in the snow and waited with their ears flat and their tails going. He served them all a bowl. They let him pass.

Stand down, the gnome said pleasantly, and when the bandits laughed, she twisted the valve on the thermos she had been quietly heating for the last thirty seconds. The jet of scalding bisque caught three of them across the chest. The other two ran. She wiped down her bandolier, set up her stand, and had customers within the hour.

The paladin would have died on that hillside if the elf had not found her. She did not have a spell slot left. What she had was a thermos of bone broth she had been slow-cooking since dawn, and hands that had served soup in worse conditions than this. The paladin drank. Her wounds closed. She never forgot the taste.

The Class

A soup savant is not just a cook. They are the entire operation — sourcing ingredients, developing the recipe, crafting the vessel, building the stand, running the commerce, and moving on when the town has had enough. There is no step of the soup business that a savant handles carelessly, because no step is beneath them and no step can be trusted to someone who does not care as much as they do.

To reach that ideal, a soup savant learns everything. They study alchemical theory to understand how heat transforms a stock. They practice conjuration to build the perfect extradimensional pantry. They master herbalism, divination, and even a little enchantment — not for its own sake, but because the perfect soup might require any of it.

A soup savant's toolkit is purposeful and specific. They do not cast spells in the traditional sense. They cook them. Their grimoire is a cookbook. Their spell slots are thermoses, prepared in advance and carried on a bandolier. They weaponize soup, heal with it, charm with it, and build a traveling business around it.

Quick Build

Put your highest ability score in Charisma, followed by Constitution or Dexterity. Choose the Guild Artisan background. For your starting instant soups, choose Bad Batch and Hot N' Ready. For your 1st-level recipes, begin with Chicken Noodle and Magic Mushroom.

Key Features

1st Level
Soupcooking
Your cookbook holds magical soup recipes. Your thermoses function as spell slots — prepared in advance, carried on a bandolier, and expended when you cook a recipe. Charisma governs your Soup save DC and attack rolls.
1st Level
Soup Cabinet
As an action, conjure a set of cabinet doors on any solid surface — a wall, a stone, even a tree. Inside: an extradimensional space stocked with ingredients, spices, and everything you need. Expands at 5th, 10th, and 15th level.
2nd Level
Culinary Edge
Enter a flow state of pure culinary focus. Roll a culinary die (d4 at 2nd level, scaling to d12 at 20th) and add the result to a damage or healing roll from a soup cooked this turn. Regains on long rest.
2nd Level
Entrepreneur's Expertise
Choose one Charisma-based skill you are proficient in. Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check using that skill. Because the soup business rewards confidence.
3rd Level
Soup of the Day
When you finish a long rest, roll on the Soup of the Day table to determine a secret enhancement. Apply it to a number of your thermoses equal to a culinary die roll. At 9th level, roll twice and choose which result applies to each group.
5th Level
Gritty Gut
Years of tasting catastrophically wrong experiments have hardened your constitution. You have advantage on saving throws against ingested hazards — poison, disease, and anything you probably should not have sampled.
6th Level
Trained Tongue
Your palate is a precision instrument. Advantage on Perception checks relying on taste or smell. You can identify any nonmagical food or substance by taste alone, and automatically know whether something you are about to consume is safe.
7th Level
Pop-up Stand
You have mastered the art of setting up shop anywhere. Given 4 hours and basic lumber, you can construct a fully operational covered soup stand — complete with a prep area, display counter, and signage. Open for business.
10th Level
Soup Kitchen
Your extradimensional pantry expands into a full 10×10×10 ft. kitchen — furnished with a magical stone hearth that burns without fuel, a long preparation counter, and racks of hanging cookware. Home, wherever you are.
14th Level
Divinely Delicious Soup
Designate a number of thermoses equal to your Charisma modifier as Divinely Delicious. These soups gain enhanced effects and force creatures that consume them to make a Wisdom saving throw or suffer disadvantage on their next save.
18th Level
Interdimensional Cauldron
Cook a Planar Broth over 1 hour. Pour it onto a solid surface and a shimmering portal opens — large enough for a Medium creature, connecting your location to a plane of existence you choose. The portal remains open for 10 minutes.
20th Level
Legendary Chef
Your Charisma increases by 2 (max 22). Your culinary die becomes a d12 with five dice. Once per long rest, cook your Magnum Opus: a legendary soup that doubles all healing from your recipes for 24 hours and grants immunity to frightened and charmed.

Starting Equipment

You begin with a magical soup cookbook, a bandolier of magical thermoses, a butcher's knife (functions as a dagger), a net, leather armor, and a pack containing cook's utensils, alchemist's supplies, a hatchet, and a tinderbox.

Creating Your Soup Savant

When creating a soup savant, consider when and where they first developed their love of soup. When did your character really commit to making the best soup possible, and who did they acquire their interdisciplinary training from? Were they apprenticed to a traveling chef who doubled as a hedge wizard? Did they stumble upon an ancient cookbook in a crumbling library? Or did they discover, one cold winter's night, that a bowl of their own soup made them feel something they had never felt before?

One question every soup savant must answer is how they became capable of storing ingredients in an extradimensional space. Did a deity reward their passion? Did they make a deal with a powerful spellcaster? The answer shapes how your character relates to the magic they wield — whether with reverence, casual ownership, or lingering debt.
