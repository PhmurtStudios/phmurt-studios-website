Soup Savant
A Homebrew Class

Every soup savant is chasing the same thing: a soup so perfect that anyone who tastes it would weep — not from sadness, but from the sudden and overwhelming sense that they have finally tasted exactly what they needed. No savant has cooked it yet. Every one of them believes they will be first.

Class Overview

The Soup Savant is a full-progression spellcaster devoted to crafting soups of extraordinary power. Through culinary arts and arcane knowledge, the Savant learns to transmute humble broths into vessels of pure magic. Some seek to perfect a single recipe; others chase the impossible dream of a soup so complete it would move the gods themselves to tears.

Class Features

1st Level

Soup Crafting

You understand the fundamental laws of culinary transmutation. You know two cantrips of your choice from the Soup Savant spell list. You learn additional cantrips at higher levels, as shown in the Class Progression Table.

When you cast a 1st-level spell that deals damage or restores hit points, you can roll a d6 and add that result to the spell's damage or healing. This increases to a d8 at 5th level, d10 at 9th level, and d12 at 13th level. You can use this ability a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Thermos Focus

You acquire or craft a thermos — an enchanted cooking vessel that serves as your spellcasting focus. Your thermos is always with you, protected by your culinary arts. If it is lost, you can perform a 1-hour ritual to attune to a new thermos. You can have only one thermos at a time, but you can craft multiple spares.

2nd Level

Culinary Edge

When you take the Attack action, you can enter a culinary flow state. You can add your Charisma modifier to the damage of one weapon attack or spell attack you make this turn. Additionally, when you cast a spell that deals damage or restores hit points, you can roll your culinary die (the same die used in Soup Crafting) and add the result to the spell's effect. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum once), regaining all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Soup Style

At 2nd level, you commit yourself to a culinary philosophy. Choose one of the three Soup Styles: Broth Militant, Bisque of Restoration, or Consommé of the Mind. Your choice grants you features at 2nd, 6th, 10th, and 14th levels.

3rd Level

Soup Stand

Your culinary business becomes portable. You can spend 1 hour to set up or break down a magical soup stand, which occupies a 10-foot-by-10-foot space. Your stand can hold up to 10 gallons of prepared soup. You can maintain knowledge of and teleport to any soup stand you've created that still exists. If your stand is destroyed, you can create a new one during a long rest, but you lose the destroyed one permanently.

While you're within your soup stand, you gain a +2 bonus to saving throws and spell attack rolls. Once per day, while at your stand, you can serve a bowl of specially prepared soup to a creature. That creature must make a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + your Charisma modifier + your proficiency bonus) or be charmed by you for 1 hour or until the charm is broken.

4th Level

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th levels, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

5th Level

Font of Flavor

You've mastered the essence of taste. When you prepare spells, you can prepare a number of soups equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum one) as rituals. These are 1st-level spells you know. Once per day, you can serve one prepared soup to a creature as a bonus action, and the spell takes effect as though you had cast it. Any spell components (except a thermos focus) must still be provided by the consumer.

6th Level

Soup Style Feature

You gain a feature from your chosen Soup Style.

7th Level

Interdimensional Pantry

You've discovered a pocket dimension that exists within your thermos. Your thermos can now hold up to 500 pounds of dry goods, ingredients, and prepared soups without gaining weight. When you finish a long rest, you can prepare up to 10 servings of soup from your pantry that will stay fresh indefinitely. These soups count against your preparation limits and can be shared with allies.

9th Level

Culinary Mastery

You've achieved mastery over basic culinary arts. When you cast a soup-based spell, you can gain resistance to one of the following damage types until the end of your next turn: fire, cold, poison, or psychic. Additionally, you can create a minor magical effect when you cook: you can clean, warm, or cool one object as an action, or you can light a campfire using only herbs and imagination.

10th Level

Soup Style Feature

You gain a feature from your chosen Soup Style.

11th Level

Traveling Market

Your reputation spreads far and wide. You can now maintain up to three Soup Stands. When you create a stand, you can choose to make it a public market or a private stand. At a public market, intelligent creatures can arrive to purchase soups of your choice. A simple healing soup costs 1 gp; more powerful recipes cost more. You gain all coin spent at your markets.

13th Level

Recipe of Wonder

You've developed a signature recipe of legendary quality. Choose one spell of 3rd level or lower that you know. You can cast this spell once using a Soup Savant spell slot without expending the slot if you use this feature. You can't do so again until you finish a long rest. Additionally, when you serve your signature recipe to a creature, that creature gains advantage on its next attack roll, ability check, or saving throw made within 1 minute.

14th Level

Soup Style Feature

You gain a feature from your chosen Soup Style.

15th Level

Essence of Flavor

You've distilled the purest essence of flavor into your art. When you cast a spell using a spell slot, you gain temporary hit points equal to the spell's level + your Charisma modifier. Additionally, when a creature drinks one of your soups and regains hit points from it, that creature gains advantage on the next Wisdom saving throw it makes within 1 minute.

17th Level

Supreme Broth

Your soups now carry supreme power. When you cast a spell that restores hit points, add your proficiency bonus to the amount restored. When you cast a spell that deals damage, add your proficiency bonus to the damage roll. Additionally, soups created at this level last indefinitely unless spoiled.

18th Level

Culinary Intuition

You've honed your senses to perceive the very essence of cuisine and magic. You can sense the presence of any magical aura within 1 mile of you, and you know the general direction and type of magic (transmutation, divination, etc.). Additionally, you can use your thermos as a spell-casting focus for any spellcaster, not just yourself.

20th Level — Capstone

Legendary Chef

You have achieved legendary status. Your Soup Style feature has reached its pinnacle. You can use your Culinary Edge feature an unlimited number of times. Additionally, when you finish a short or long rest, you can create one legendary soup of your choice. This soup can have a rarity of Rare or lower. Legendary soups you create have double potency: they grant advantage on two saving throws instead of one, or restore 2d8 additional hit points, or deal 2d8 additional damage. You can maintain up to 5 Soup Stands, and they never require maintenance. When you cast a spell, you can add your Charisma modifier to the saving throw DC and spell attack rolls.

Soup Styles

The following sections describe the three major Soup Styles a Soup Savant can choose from.

Broth Militant

Broth Militant savants focus on the offensive applications of their craft. They weaponize heat, harness the power of spices, and turn simple broths into instruments of combat.

2nd Level — Scalding Strike

When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can spend one Culinary Edge use to deal an additional 1d6 fire damage. This damage increases to 2d6 at 5th level, 3d6 at 10th level, and 4d6 at 14th level.

6th Level — Spiced Arsenal

You learn three martial spells of your choice from the Soup Savant list. When you cast a spell that deals fire or poison damage, you can add your Charisma modifier to the damage roll.

10th Level — Boiling Point

When you cast a spell that deals fire damage, you can choose to maximize the damage dice instead of rolling them. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all uses when you finish a long rest.

14th Level — Warrior's Reduction

You learn to create soups that enhance physical combat. When you or an ally drinks one of your specially prepared soups, they gain a +2 bonus to melee attack rolls and melee damage rolls for 1 hour. Once per day, you can prepare such a soup.

Bisque of Restoration

Healers and nurturers follow the path of Bisque. They craft soups of mending, sustenance, and hope, turning creamy broths into vessels of salvation.

2nd Level — Healing Touch

When you cast a spell that restores hit points, you can spend one Culinary Edge use to add 1d6 additional healing. This increases to 2d6 at 5th level, 3d6 at 10th level, and 4d6 at 14th level.

6th Level — Empath's Broth

You learn three support spells from the Soup Savant list. When you cast a spell that restores hit points or grants temporary hit points, you can add your Charisma modifier to the amount granted.

10th Level — Life Essence

When you cast a spell that restores hit points, you can choose to roll damage dice twice and use the higher result for each die. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all uses when you finish a long rest.

14th Level — Guardian's Nourishment

You create soups that mend wounds and bolster the spirit. When an ally drinks one of your specially prepared soups, they regain 2d6 + your Charisma modifier hit points and gain resistance to one damage type of your choice until the end of their next long rest. Once per day, you can prepare such a soup.

Consommé of the Mind

Mystics and scholars pursue Consommé. They brew soups infused with ancient wisdom, manipulation, and the subtle arts of enchantment.

2nd Level — Thought Flavoring

When you cast a spell that requires a saving throw, you can spend one Culinary Edge use to add 1d6 to your spell's save DC. This increases to 2d6 at 5th level, 3d6 at 10th level, and 4d6 at 14th level.

6th Level — Mysterious Concoction

You learn three enchantment or divination spells from the Soup Savant list. When you cast a spell that requires an Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma saving throw, you can add your Charisma modifier to the save DC.

10th Level — Mind's Depth

When you cast a spell that forces a creature to make a saving throw, you can choose to have it roll the save with disadvantage. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all uses when you finish a long rest.

14th Level — Sage's Brew

You create soups of transcendent knowledge. When a creature drinks one of your specially prepared soups, they gain advantage on Intelligence (Arcana), Intelligence (History), and Wisdom (Insight) checks for 1 hour, and they can add your proficiency bonus to these checks. Once per day, you can prepare such a soup.
