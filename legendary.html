Legendary Soups
Recipes of the Mightiest Creatures

Legendary soups occupy a category beyond even 7th-level recipes. Each one requires a specific ingredient that can only be obtained by defeating one of the most powerful creatures in existence. A soup savant who manages to harvest such an ingredient and cook it successfully joins a list of perhaps a dozen people in the history of the world.

Rules

Legendary soups do not require a thermos. They are cooked in a full cauldron and require a minimum of 8 hours of uninterrupted preparation unless stated otherwise. They cannot be enhanced by Soup of the Day, Culinary Edge, or Divinely Delicious. Each legendary soup can only be cooked once per ingredient harvested — the ingredient is consumed in the cooking and cannot be recovered.

Your Dungeon Master must approve the harvesting of a legendary ingredient and determine what that process requires. In most cases, you must have dealt the killing blow, participated in the final battle, or been present at the creature's death. Some ingredients require specific handling within a short time after the creature's death.

The Recipes

Tarrasque Tail Consomé
From the tail of the world's most unkillable creature

There is one Tarrasque. It has been killed before, barely and at tremendous cost, and each time it has come back. When it finally stays dead, the tail is the first thing a soup savant goes for. The resulting consomé is the clearest, most golden broth any savant has ever seen.

Legendary Ingredient
Tarrasque tail marrow, sawn within 1 hour of death before regeneration seals the wound
Cooking Time
8 hours over open flame. The marrow does not liquefy until the sixth hour.
Serves
1 creature (permanent effects)
Effects
+50 max HP · Resistance to nonmagical bludgeoning, piercing, slashing · Regenerate 10 HP per turn · Immunity to frightened
Beholder Eye Bisque
From the central eye of a creature that hates everything it sees

A beholder's central eye is the organ of its antimagic cone. It is also, it turns out, the most flavorful part of the creature. The eye socket contains a saline humor of extraordinary magical density, and when slow-roasted in its own fluid, it produces a bisque that tastes of ozone and, strangely, of comfort.

Legendary Ingredient
Central eye removed whole within 10 minutes of death. Antimagic humor must be skimmed and discarded throughout cooking.
Cooking Time
12 hours, slow roast with constant skimming required
Serves
1 creature (permanent effects)
